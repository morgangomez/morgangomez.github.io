Locked Down

Photography, 3D

This project shows how I felt like during the first lockdown in France, which lasted 3 months from march to june 2020.

I really felt overwhelmed by all the medias and screens that were surrounding me. I was living to see the next "breaking news" or the latest "COVID-19 numbers". What did the coronavirus do today? Is there any more death? What is the government doing? Who? What? Why? Where? I was addicted to 24/7 media channels. Since the beggining of the lockdown, I felt like a lab rat, studied, observed, watched by all these screens that were ready to yell their latest #BreakingNews at me.
My state of mind was very unstable and changed every other days. That's why I made a series of photos where each picture shows a particular state of mind that I was in at the time: indifference, boredom, curiosity, obsession, oppression, surrendering, apathy, hope, freedom. I wanted to make sure that the boudaries between what was in my head and what was reality were as blurry as possible.

This is the story of how the medias and screens locked me down, and how I broke free from my own obsession.
